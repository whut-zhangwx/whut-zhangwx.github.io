The lakes abound with fish.

Mon 9/19/2022

1-10

accordion

  • 手风琴

accounting

  • accounting firm 会计事务所
    accounting and economics 会计学和经济学
    a master’s degree in accounting 会计学硕士学位
    a bachelor’s degree in accounting 会计学学士学位

accredit

  • be accredited 被官方认可
    full accreditation 完整认可

  • accredit sth to sb 将某事归功于某人；认为（某事为某人所说、所做）
    accredit sb with sth 将某事归功于某人；认为（某事为某人所说、所做）
    We accredit the invention of the electric light to Adison.

  • accredit sb to … 委任，委派

accumulate

  • accumulate a million dollars
    accumulate useful experience

  • Lead can accumulate in the body

accuse

  • accuse sb (of sth)
    to accuse sb of murder/theft 控告某人谋杀╱盗窃

  • stand accused of 被谴责（formally）

acid

  • acetic acid 乙酸；醋酸；CH₃COOH
    amino acid 氨基酸
    citric acid 柠檬酸
    hydrochloric acid 盐酸 ；氯化氢HCl（an acid containing hydrogen and chlorine）；
  • acid rain 酸雨
  • alkali 碱

acidity

  • low/medium/high acidity 低/中/高 酸度
  • soil acidity 土壤酸度

acoustic

  • acoustic guitar 原声吉他；木吉他
  • acoustic wave 声波

acquaint

  • acquaint sb/yourself with sth 使某人熟悉某事
    Please acquaint me with the facts of the case.

acquainted

  • be acquainted with sth 熟悉了解某事
    The students are already acquainted with the work of Shakespeare.
  • be acquainted with sb 与某人相识熟悉
    I am well acquainted with her.

acquiesce

  • acquiesce in 默许（often used with in and sometimes with to）
    Steve seemed to acquiesce in the decision.
    acquiesce to old age 服老

11-20

acquisition

  • language acquisition
    acquisition of knowledge

acquit

  • acquit sb (of sth) 宣判…无罪
    The jury acquitted him of murder.
  • acquit yourself well, badly, etc. ( formal )
    He acquitted himself brilliantly in the exams.

acronym

acting

acumen

acute

adamant

adapt

adaptable

adaptive
